One of the pleasures of intellectual life is purely bibliographical: serendipity in finding sources or references, intriguing citations in footnotes or the annotations of a library book, reading suggestions from colleagues. One of its sorrows is that we are not able to give many sources their due. Some merit simply a footnote or parenthetical reference while others are not immediately relevant or useful. Many nevertheless are interesting or amusing, and make an unacknowledged contribution to our academic work. Below is a hode-podge of such books or essays.
